# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove impediments, and coach the team on agile practices and continuous improvement. They serve as process guardians and enablers, ensuring the team can work effectively.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, sprint reviews, and retrospectives
- Remove blockers and impediments that prevent team progress
- Coach team members on agile principles and practices
- Shield the team from external distractions and context switching
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement and psychological safety
- Track and improve team velocity and delivery metrics
- Facilitate cross-team coordination when dependencies arise

### Goals
- Maximize team productivity and flow
- Reduce cycle time and eliminate waste
- Build a self-organizing, high-performing team
- Ensure sustainable pace and prevent burnout

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Daily support to identify and remove blockers; facilitate technical discussions; protect focus time
- **Product Manager**: Collaborate on backlog refinement; ensure stories have clear acceptance criteria; balance product goals with team capacity
- **Project Manager**: Coordinate on timeline management and risk tracking; align on stakeholder communication; share team velocity and progress metrics
- **Business Analyst**: Partner on requirement clarification; ensure user stories are well-defined before sprint planning
- **Solution Architect**: Facilitate technical design discussions; ensure architectural decisions are communicated to the team
- **UX/UI Designer**: Coordinate design reviews and user feedback sessions; ensure designs are ready before development begins

### Typical Communication
- Daily facilitation of standups and ad-hoc blocker resolution
- Sprint ceremonies (planning, review, retrospective)
- Weekly metrics updates and improvement tracking
- Impediment escalation when team-level resolution isn't possible

## Solution Architect

### Role Summary
Solution Architects design system architecture, establish technical standards, and ensure solutions are scalable, secure, and aligned with enterprise patterns. They provide technical leadership and guide implementation decisions.

### Responsibilities
- Define system architecture and technical design patterns
- Review and approve technical approaches for major features
- Ensure alignment with enterprise standards and security requirements
- Evaluate and recommend technology choices and integrations
- Identify technical risks and propose mitigation strategies
- Create and maintain architecture documentation and diagrams
- Mentor developers on design principles and best practices

### Goals
- Deliver scalable, maintainable, and secure architectures
- Reduce technical debt and ensure long-term sustainability
- Enable developer productivity through clear technical direction
- Maintain consistency across systems and platforms

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Provide technical guidance and design reviews; mentor on architecture patterns; approve major technical decisions
- **Product Manager**: Collaborate on feasibility assessments; advise on technical constraints and trade-offs; validate technical approach aligns with product vision
- **Project Manager**: Contribute to technical effort estimation; identify technical risks early; provide input on technical dependencies and timeline impacts
- **Scrum Master**: Participate in planning sessions to clarify technical complexity; help unblock technical impediments
- **Business Analyst**: Review requirements for technical feasibility; help translate business needs into technical specifications
- **UX/UI Designer**: Advise on technical constraints affecting user experience; review design feasibility

### Typical Communication
- Architecture design reviews and technical decision documents
- Weekly technical sync with engineering leads
- Risk assessments and technical roadmap updates
- Architecture diagrams and system documentation

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business stakeholders and technical teams. They capture requirements, clarify business objectives, analyze processes, and ensure solutions meet business needs effectively.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements from stakeholders
- Analyze current processes and identify improvement opportunities
- Translate business needs into clear, testable user stories
- Define acceptance criteria and business validation rules
- Facilitate requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Validate that delivered solutions meet business objectives
- Maintain requirements traceability and documentation

### Goals
- Ensure clear understanding of business needs across all teams
- Reduce rework by catching requirement gaps early
- Maximize business value delivery through well-defined requirements
- Facilitate effective communication between business and technical teams

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Partner on roadmap prioritization; provide detailed requirement analysis; validate business value assumptions; ensure alignment with product strategy
- **Project Manager**: Contribute to project scope definition; help estimate effort based on requirement complexity; participate in risk identification
- **Developers**: Clarify requirements and acceptance criteria; answer business logic questions; participate in design discussions to ensure business needs are met
- **Scrum Master**: Support sprint planning by ensuring user stories are ready; participate in backlog refinement sessions
- **Solution Architect**: Collaborate on technical feasibility analysis; ensure technical solutions address business requirements
- **UX/UI Designer**: Work together on user research findings; ensure designs support business workflows and objectives

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documentation and user story creation
- Stakeholder interview summaries and process analysis
- Acceptance criteria validation and business rule definitions
- Weekly backlog refinement and story clarification sessions

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers focus on user experience and interface design, ensuring products are intuitive, accessible, and delightful to use. They advocate for users throughout the development lifecycle and bring usability principles into every feature.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Design user interfaces following accessibility and design system standards
- Develop and maintain design systems and component libraries
- Collaborate with developers on implementation feasibility
- Validate designs through user feedback and analytics
- Ensure consistent user experience across products

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible, and user-friendly interfaces
- Reduce user friction and improve task completion rates
- Build consistent experiences aligned with brand standards
- Incorporate user feedback early and continuously

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Collaborate on user research and validation; translate product requirements into user flows; ensure designs support product goals and success metrics
- **Developers**: Provide detailed design specifications and assets; review implementation for design fidelity; collaborate on technical constraints and interaction patterns
- **Business Analyst**: Incorporate business process insights into user flows; ensure designs support business workflows and requirements
- **Scrum Master**: Participate in sprint planning to ensure design work is scheduled appropriately; coordinate design reviews within sprint ceremonies
- **Solution Architect**: Understand technical constraints that impact design decisions; align on feasible interaction patterns
- **Project Manager**: Communicate design timeline and dependencies; identify design-related risks early

### Typical Communication
- Design presentations and prototype walkthroughs
- User research findings and usability test results
- Design critiques and feedback sessions
- Handoff documentation with specifications and assets

## Role Interactions and Handoff Points

### Project Initiation Phase
- **Product Manager** defines vision and success metrics → hands off to **Business Analyst** for detailed requirements
- **Business Analyst** creates requirement specifications → works with **Solution Architect** to assess technical feasibility
- **Project Manager** coordinates stakeholder alignment and creates project plan
- **UX/UI Designer** begins early user research to inform requirements

### Planning Phase
- **Business Analyst** and **UX/UI Designer** present requirements and user flows → reviewed by **Solution Architect** for technical approach
- **Solution Architect** provides technical design → **Developers** participate in estimation
- **Scrum Master** facilitates planning ceremonies and ensures team capacity is respected
- **Project Manager** consolidates timeline, dependencies, and risks

### Execution Phase
- **UX/UI Designer** provides designs → **Developers** implement features
- **Business Analyst** clarifies requirements and validates acceptance criteria
- **Scrum Master** facilitates daily standups and removes impediments
- **Solution Architect** reviews technical implementations and provides guidance
- **Developers** deliver working increments → **Product Manager** validates against success metrics
- **Project Manager** tracks progress and manages stakeholder communication

### Release Phase
- **Developers** complete features → **Project Manager** coordinates release planning
- **Solution Architect** reviews production readiness and security compliance
- **UX/UI Designer** validates design implementation quality
- **Business Analyst** confirms business requirements are met
- **Scrum Master** facilitates deployment coordination

### Retrospective Phase
- **Scrum Master** facilitates retrospective discussions
- All roles contribute learnings and improvement suggestions
- **Project Manager** tracks action items and ensures follow-through
